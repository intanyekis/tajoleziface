About East Allegheny

East Allegheny, also known as Deutschtown, is a vibrant neighborhood located on Pittsburgh’s North Side. With its unique charm and rich history, it has become a beloved community for residents and visitors alike. East Allegheny is characterized by its diverse population, beautiful architecture, and a strong sense of community.

As part of Pittsburgh’s North Central Neighborhoods, East Allegheny is represented by the council member for District 1. History and Development of the Area

The roots of East Allegheny can be traced back to 1783 when the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania established a 3000-acre tract of land north of the confluence of the Allegheny and Ohio Rivers. The visionary John Redick created the initial town plan for Allegheny City in the following year, which included 36 city blocks surrounded by a common grazing area.

Over time, the common grazing area transformed into the picturesque Allegheny Commons, a park that serves as a gathering place for the community. The area just east of the park, originally designated for farming in Redick’s plan, eventually became the vibrant neighborhood we know today as East Allegheny.
